Building in public is a commitment to showing your work — not just the polished results, but the process, the false starts, and the half-finished ideas.

## What "building in public" means to me

It means posting about a project before it's done. It means sharing what I'm learning, not just what I've learned. It means being okay with being wrong in front of people.

## Why bother?

1. **Accountability** — Saying you're going to do something publicly makes it real
2. **Serendipity** — People find your work and you find each other
3. **Learning** — Explaining something helps you understand it better
4. **Giving back** — Others might learn from your process, not just your outcomes

## The fear

The biggest barrier is embarrassment. What if this project never ships? What if my code is bad? What if nobody cares?

All of those things will happen. That's fine.

The alternative — keeping everything private until it's perfect — means most things never ship at all.

## Let's go

This blog is my first step. I'll be writing about what I'm working on, what I'm reading, and what I'm thinking. Rough edges and all.
